The Modern Landscape Painting

A critique on the modern landscape painting scene and how it demonstrates the far-reaching influences of digital art

Modern Painting



Digital painting has been the crux of the art and design industry of the twenty-first century. No longer is it required to spend hundreds on painting tools, canvases, paints and brushes to be able to produce print-quality artwork. In this day and age, one only needs a computer, a tablet and enough talent and experience to be noticed online.

In addition, the entire process of gaining exposure has been simplified. No longer is it mandatory for up and coming artists to slog in town exhibitions or compete against higher tier artists for a deal in an auction house. People can simply post their work online and get exposure almost immediately.


'46' by gydw1n


The scene is competitive still, but much less gruelling that what artists might experience only a few decades ago. Today the market is wider and the stage is larger, and with that the modern digital art took of quickly.


Some more traditional artists however have argued that since digital art does not employ the regular and traditional tools of creating art, it is therefore a sort of mimicry of art, or a lesser for of the media. In reality, art has always been about mimicry and using the most unusual and unorthodox techniques to bedazzle and awe its viewers. Art is never restricted to one method or material.

Good artists copy; great artists steal.

Pablo Picasso
